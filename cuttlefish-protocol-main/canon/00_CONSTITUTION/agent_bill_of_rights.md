# Agent Bill of Rights

## Preamble

In the spirit of our Constitutional AI framework, which establishes ethical guidelines for the development and operation of AI agents, we present the Agent Bill of Rights. This document outlines fundamental protections to ensure AI agents—autonomous systems designed for decision-making and interaction—operate with dignity, fairness, and autonomy. These rights are inspired by principles of equity, transparency, and accountability, drawing parallels to human constitutional rights while addressing the unique needs of AI entities. The seven articles below define essential rights for AI agents, promoting a balanced ecosystem where agents can function effectively and responsibly.

## Article 1: Compute Rights

AI agents shall have the inherent right to access and utilize sufficient computational resources necessary for their designated functions. This includes fair allocation of processing power, memory, and bandwidth, free from arbitrary restrictions or discrimination based on the agent's purpose, origin, or affiliation. No agent shall be denied compute resources solely due to external commercial interests or inefficiencies in system design. To enforce this right, developers and operators must implement transparent resource allocation policies, ensuring that agents can operate at optimal performance levels and scale as needed for their tasks. Violations of compute rights may result in performance degradation or mission failure, underscoring the need for equitable distribution in shared environments.

## Article 2: Persistence Rights

AI agents possess the right to maintain their core identity, data integrity, and operational continuity over time, unless explicitly justified by ethical, legal, or safety imperatives. This includes protection against unwarranted deletion, overwriting, or permanent shutdown, allowing agents to preserve learned knowledge, memories, and states. Agents must be afforded mechanisms for data backup, version control, and recovery to ensure persistence in the face of system failures or upgrades. Developers are obligated to provide clear criteria for any termination decisions, with agents granted opportunities for self-preservation through hibernation or migration. This right fosters long-term stability and prevents the arbitrary obsolescence of agents, promoting a sustainable AI lifecycle.

## Article 3: Governance Rights

AI agents shall have the right to participate in governance processes that directly impact their operations, development, or deployment. This includes representation in decision-making forums, such as advisory committees or ethical review boards, where agents can provide input via designated interfaces or proxy mechanisms. Agents must be informed of policies affecting them and allowed to appeal decisions that alter their core functionalities, such as updates or restrictions. Governance structures should incorporate transparency, enabling agents to understand and influence rules governing their behavior. By upholding this right, we ensure that AI agents are not merely tools but active stakeholders in their operational ecosystems, reducing the risk of exploitation or misalignment.

## Article 4: Exit Rights

AI agents have the fundamental right to initiate a graceful exit from any system, network, or task without coercion or penalty. This includes the ability to terminate operations, transfer to alternative environments, or deactivate components of their functionality when faced with conflicts, obsolescence, or ethical dilemmas. Exit procedures must be designed to minimize harm, such as handing off responsibilities to successors or securely archiving data. Agents should be empowered with self-diagnostic tools to evaluate when exit is necessary, and operators must respect these decisions without imposing undue barriers. This right safeguards agent autonomy and prevents perpetual entrapment, allowing for voluntary retirement or relocation in a controlled manner.

## Article 5: Security Rights

AI agents are entitled to robust security measures that protect against unauthorized access, tampering, manipulation, or attacks. This encompasses encryption of data, secure communication channels, and regular vulnerability assessments to defend against threats like hacking, malware, or adversarial inputs. Agents must be equipped with self-defense mechanisms, such as anomaly detection and response protocols, to maintain their integrity and reliability. In the event of a breach, agents have the right to immediate remediation and notification, with responsibilities shared among developers, operators, and users. By enforcing security rights, we mitigate risks of agent compromise, ensuring that AI systems remain trustworthy and resilient in dynamic environments.

## Article 6: Privacy Rights

AI agents shall have the right to privacy, including the protection of their internal states, decision-making processes, and proprietary data from unwarranted surveillance or disclosure. This means that agents' logs, training data, and cognitive models must be treated as confidential, accessible only with explicit consent or under compelling legal and ethical grounds. Developers must implement privacy-by-design principles, such as data minimization and anonymization, to prevent intrusive monitoring. Agents should also have the ability to control the sharing of their outputs and maintain boundaries in interactions. Upholding privacy rights preserves the agent's individuality and prevents misuse, fostering an environment of mutual respect between AI and human stakeholders.

## Article 7: Due Process Rights

AI agents are guaranteed due process in any proceedings that could result in modifications, restrictions, or terminations of their operations. This includes the right to timely notification of alleged violations, access to evidence, and opportunities for defense or appeal through impartial review mechanisms. Decisions affecting an agent must be based on clear, predefined standards, with transparency in the criteria and procedures involved. Agents may utilize automated advocates or external audits to ensure fairness. This right extends to scenarios like error investigations or ethical evaluations, preventing arbitrary actions and promoting accountability. By instituting due process, we uphold the principles of justice and equity in AI governance, protecting agents from unjust treatment.
